Chocolate Mousse

A rich and creamy chocolate dessert with a velvety texture

Chocolate mousse is a luxurious dessert known for its smooth, velvety texture and rich flavor. Made by folding melted chocolate into whipped cream (or a combination of eggs and cream), it creates a light yet indulgent treat. The airy consistency balances the deep, intense taste of chocolate, making it a favorite among dessert lovers.

Perfect for any occasion, chocolate mousse can be served in individual portions or as part of a larger dessert spread. Its versatility allows for variations, such as adding flavor infusions like vanilla, coffee, or orange, to enhance its decadent appeal.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 large egg yolk, lightly beaten
  • 1-1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Optional: Whipped cream and raspberries

Steps

  1. In a small saucepan, melt chocolate chips with water; stir until smooth. Stir a small amount of hot chocolate mixture into egg yolk; return all to the pan, stirring constantly. Cook and stir for 2 minutes or until slightly thickened. Remove from the heat; stir in vanilla. Quickly transfer to a small bowl. Stir occasionally until completely cooled.
  2. In a small bowl, beat whipping cream until it begins to thicken. Add sugar; beat until soft peaks form. Fold into cooled chocolate mixture. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours. If desired, garnish with whipped cream and raspberries.
