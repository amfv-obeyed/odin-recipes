Whipped Shortbread Cookies

A light, buttery, and melt-in-your-mouth, delicate crumb cookie

Whipped shortbread cookies are a melt-in-your-mouth treat with a light, airy texture. Made with simple ingredients like butter, flour, and sugar, they have a delicate crumb that almost dissolves on the tongue.

These cookies are perfect for tea time or holiday celebrations, offering a subtle, buttery flavor that's both sweet and satisfying. Their melt-in-your-mouth consistency makes them a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups butter, softened
  • 1-1/2 cups confectioners' sugar, sifted
  • 4-1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1-1/2 cups cornstarch
  • Nonpareils and/or halved candied cherries

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, cream butter and confectioners' sugar until light and fluffy, 3-4 minutes. Gradually add flour and cornstarch, beating until well blended.
  2. With hands lightly dusted with additional cornstarch, roll dough into 1-in. balls. Place 1 in. apart on ungreased baking sheets. Press lightly with a floured fork. Top with nonpareils or cherry halves.
  3. Bake at 300Â° until bottoms are lightly browned, 20-22 minutes. Cool for 5 minutes before removing from pans to wire racks.
